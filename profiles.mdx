## What is a profile?

A profile is the main record for a person in Campium, linked to their family or account. It brings together their contact details, forms, notes, payments, medical details, and more in one place.

Use profiles when you need to look up someone quickly, answer questions from families, or update information during the season.

<Callout kind="info" collapsed="false">
  Profile changes affect the live family account. Double-check sensitive details like balances, medications, and contact info before you move on.
</Callout>

## How to open a profile

Use the admin search tools or family views to open an individual’s profile.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Search for the person" icon="search" title-type="p">
    From the admin dashboard, use your usual search or list view (such as Families, Campers, or Staff) to find the person you want. Look for their name in the results.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Open the profile" icon="arrow-right" title-type="p">
    Click the person’s name anywhere it appears as a link. This opens the Profiles screen for that person in a new view.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Confirm you have the right record" icon="check-circle" title-type="p">
    Check the name, family information, and contact details in the header. If something does not match, go back and open a different person with a similar name.
  </Step>
</Steps>

When the profile opens successfully, you see the person’s name at the top, a status badge, and a row of tabs such as Data Views, Notes, Sales, Forms, Emails, Files, and more.

## What you can do from a profile

The Profiles screen is your one-stop view for everything tied to that person’s camp history. From a single profile, you can:

- See and update custom fields and answers for that person.

- Add notes.

- Review sales history, payments, upcoming payments, and balances.

- View, approve, or reset forms for different seasons.

- Review all emails sent to or about this person.

- Upload and manage files related to the camper or staff member.

- Manage medications and related details, if your site has this feature.

- Review activity history such as logins, signatures, and pickup permissions, when available.

<Callout kind="tip" collapsed="false">
  Profiles stay with the person across seasons. Use the same profile to see past years, current programs, and upcoming registrations.
</Callout>

## Profile page layout

The Profiles screen is split into a header at the top and tabs below.

### Header area

The header gives you a quick snapshot of who you are looking at:

- **Name and status**: Shows the person’s full name and a badge that reflects their current status (for example, active or inactive).

- **Contact information**: Displays primary contact details such as phone number and email address so you can reach out quickly.

- **Family members**: Shows links to other profiles in the same family or account, so you can move between siblings or guardians without going back to search.

- **Profile photo**: Lets you upload, crop, rotate, or delete a profile photo so staff can recognize the person easily.

When you change fields in the header or on many tabs, Campium often saves as you go. If a save is needed, you may see a button or message next to the field or section.

<Callout kind="info" collapsed="false">
  Some buttons and fields in the header may be hidden based on your staff role. If you expect to see something, check with a system admin about your permissions.
</Callout>

#### Active Cart indicator

The **Active Cart** area in the header shows that the family currently has items in progress in their online cart that they have not finished checking out.

Use the Active Cart button or indicator when a family calls with questions about something they started but did not complete:

- Open the Active Cart to see which sessions, add-ons, or other items are sitting in the cart.

- Refer to the cart contents while you answer questions about pricing, availability, or what they were trying to register for.

- Confirm whether the family still needs those items before you point them to another registration path.

Cart contents can change at any time as families add or remove items or complete checkout. Treat the Active Cart as a snapshot of what is in progress right now, not a final enrollment or payment record.

<Callout kind="tip" collapsed="false">
  If a family says they finished checkout but you still see an Active Cart, ask them to confirm they reached a final confirmation screen and check the Sales tab to verify whether charges actually completed.
</Callout>

### Actions menu

The **Actions** button in the profile header opens a menu of tools that help you communicate with the family, manage invoices and balances, and update family relationships or access. The exact options you see depend on your staff permissions and whether the profile has usable contact details such as an email address or mobile number.

Use the Actions menu when you need to do something specific for this person or family, rather than when you only need to view information.

<ExpandableGroup>
  <Expandable title="Send Email" default-open="false">
    **Send Email** opens a window so you can send a direct email to the person or their family from the profile.

    Use this option when you want to send a one-off message related to this person, such as a question about a form, a reminder about a payment, or a follow-up after a conversation.

    After you click **Send Email**, a composer opens where you choose the recipient, enter a subject and message, and send the email. The message usually appears later in the Email history for the profile.

    <Callout kind="info" collapsed="false">
      The **Send Email** option only appears when the profile is linked to at least one email address. If you do not see it, check the contact info in the header.
    </Callout>
  </Expandable>

  <Expandable title="Send SMS" default-open="false">
    **Send SMS** lets you send a text message to a mobile number tied to this person or their family.

    Use this when you need to reach someone quickly with a short update, such as a pickup change, late bus notice, or urgent reminder that is better by text than email.

    When you click **Send SMS**, a small window opens where you confirm the mobile number, type your message, and send it. The message may also appear in your messaging history depending on how your site is configured.

    <Callout kind="alert" collapsed="false">
      Only send SMS messages for time-sensitive or important updates and follow your camp’s policies around texting families. The **Send SMS** option may be hidden if your site does not have texting enabled or if the profile does not include a mobile number that can receive messages.
    </Callout>
  </Expandable>

  <Expandable title="View Invoices" default-open="false">
    **View Invoices** opens a list of invoices connected to this person’s family, usually focused on charges that involve this profile.

    Use it when a family asks for copies of their invoices, has questions about specific charges, or wants to understand what they owe for certain sessions.

    After clicking **View Invoices**, you typically see a new window or screen with invoice numbers, dates, amounts, and links to open each invoice. From there, you can review line items or download and share copies as your role allows.
  </Expandable>

  <Expandable title="Email Invoice" default-open="false">
    **Email Invoice** sends an invoice to the family by email directly from the profile.

    Use this when someone requests a copy of a bill, needs documentation for reimbursement, or says they never received an earlier invoice.

    When you choose **Email Invoice**, Campium prompts you to pick which invoice to send and confirm the email recipient. After you send it, the family receives an email with the invoice details or a link to view it, and the activity usually appears in the Email history for the profile.

    <Callout kind="tip" collapsed="false">
      Confirm the email address in the profile header before you use **Email Invoice**, especially if a family recently changed their contact info.
    </Callout>
  </Expandable>

  <Expandable title="Send Account Balance Email" default-open="false">
    **Send Account Balance Email** sends an email that summarizes the family’s current balance and related payment information.

    Use this option when you want to remind a family about an outstanding balance, share an updated total after an adjustment, or give them a snapshot of what they owe without going through a full invoice.

    After you click **Send Account Balance Email**, you see a confirmation window that shows which email address will receive the message and, in many setups, a preview of the balance summary. Once you confirm, the email goes out and should appear in the Email history for the profile.
  </Expandable>

  <Expandable title="Change Family Status" default-open="false">
    **Change Family Status** updates how the system categorizes the family overall, such as active, inactive, prospect, or similar labels your camp uses.

    Use this when you know the family’s relationship with camp has changed, for example when they move away, ask to be marked inactive, or you need to mark them as no longer attending future seasons.

    When you select **Change Family Status**, a small window opens where you pick the new status and often add a brief note. After you confirm, the change updates across the family record and may affect filters in lists and reports.

    <Callout kind="alert" collapsed="false">
      Changing the family status can affect how the family appears in searches, reports, and communication lists. If you are not sure which status to choose, check with a supervisor before you apply the change.
    </Callout>
  </Expandable>

  <Expandable title="New Family Member" default-open="false">
    **New Family Member** adds another person to the same family account, such as a new camper, sibling, or guardian.

    Use this when a parent reports a new camper, when you need to add a second adult to the account, or when a staff member connected to the family needs their own profile.

    After you click **New Family Member**, a form opens where you enter basic details like name, role (camper, guardian, staff, and so on), and contact info. Once you save, Campium creates a new profile that is linked to the same family so you can switch between family members from the header.
  </Expandable>

  <Expandable title="Move to Another Family" default-open="false">
    **Move to Another Family** reassigns the person to a different family account, keeping their profile but changing which family it belongs to.

    Use this when guardians change, such as after a custody update, when you discover a duplicate family that needs to be merged, or when a camper should be grouped with a different household.

    After you choose **Move to Another Family**, you see a flow where you search for or create the new family, confirm that you are moving the correct person, and then complete the move. Once the move finishes, the profile appears under the new family in searches and in the header links.

    <Callout kind="alert" collapsed="false">
      Moving someone to another family changes how their charges, forms, and communication history relate to guardians. Double-check you have the correct destination family before you confirm the move.
    </Callout>
  </Expandable>

  <Expandable title="Send Password Reset Email" default-open="false">
    **Send Password Reset Email** triggers an email that lets the family reset their portal password.

    Use this when a parent or staff member cannot sign in and asks you to send a reset link, especially if they are on the phone or at your front desk.

    When you click **Send Password Reset Email**, Campium sends a reset link to the email address tied to the family’s login and usually shows a quick confirmation message on screen. The person receiving the email follows the link to set a new password and regain access.

    <Callout kind="info" collapsed="false">
      If the email address on the account is wrong or outdated, the reset message will go to the wrong place. Confirm the contact info before you send a password reset.
    </Callout>
  </Expandable>

  <Expandable title="Delete (person)" default-open="false">
    **Delete (person)** removes the individual profile from active use in your system.

    Use this only in rare situations where a profile was created by mistake, is clearly a duplicate that should not stay active, or needs to be removed under your camp’s data-handling policies.

    After you choose **Delete (person)**, Campium prompts you to confirm the action and may show a summary of what will happen to linked information. Once you confirm, you either remove the profile from normal views or move it into a deleted or archived state, depending on your site’s configuration.

    <Callout kind="alert" collapsed="false">
      Deleting a person can have lasting effects on linked forms, financial records, and history. Follow your camp’s procedures for handling duplicates or removal requests, and only delete a profile when you are certain it is appropriate.
    </Callout>
  </Expandable>
</ExpandableGroup>

### Tabs and main content

Under the header, you see a row of tabs that organize all of the information for this person. The tab set can change based on:

- Which modules your camp uses (for example, medications).

- Which seasons or sessions are active.

- Your own staff permissions.

If a tab is missing, it usually means you do not have access to that area or your site does not use that feature.

## Tabs and sections

Each tab focuses on a different part of the person’s record. Use these sections to understand what you can do on each one.

<Tabs>
  <Tab title="All User Data" icon="list">
    The **Data Views** or **User Data** tab shows custom fields and answers for the person, such as grade, cabin preferences, or other questions from your setup.

    - Review responses collected from registrations and forms.

    - Update answers when a parent calls in a change.

    - Switch seasons or views (when available) to see how data changes over time.

    Fields on this tab often update as soon as you change them, without needing to click a separate save button.
  </Tab>

  <Tab title="Notes" icon="edit">
    The **Notes** tab holds internal comments about the person that only staff can see.

    - Add new notes for behavior, special instructions, or follow-ups.

    - Edit existing notes when you need to correct or clarify something.

    - Remove notes that are no longer relevant, according to your camp policies.

    Use clear, professional language in notes, since other staff rely on this tab to make decisions during camp.
  </Tab>

  <Tab title="Sales" icon="dollar-sign">
    The **Sales** tab shows the financial side of this person’s relationship with your camp.

    - View past sales and payments tied to this person and their family.

    - See upcoming or future payments and scheduled charges.

    - Review current balances and filter by season.

    <Callout kind="alert" collapsed="false">
      Be careful when changing anything that affects balances or payments. If you are unsure, confirm with your finance lead before making adjustments.
    </Callout>

    Filters near the top of the tab help you narrow by season so you can focus on the correct year or session.
  </Tab>

  <Tab title="Forms" icon="file-text">
    The **Forms** tab shows all forms connected to this person.

    - See which forms are complete, pending, or missing.

    - Approve, reset, or reject forms when you review submissions.

    - Filter by season to see forms related to a specific year.

    When you reset or reject a form, the family often needs to log back in to complete or correct it, so use those options thoughtfully.
  </Tab>

  <Tab title="Emails" icon="mail">
    The **Emails** tab is an email history for this person and their family.

    - Review emails sent from Campium, including mass emails, automated messages, and individual messages.

    - Confirm whether an important email was sent, when it was delivered, and in some cases whether it bounced.

    - Open individual emails to see the exact content if a family has questions about what they received.

    Use this tab to avoid sending duplicate messages and to confirm communication timelines when families say they did not receive something.

    <Callout kind="info" collapsed="false">
      Email logs may include personal contact information and message content. Only share details from this tab with people who are authorized to see the family’s communication history.
    </Callout>
  </Tab>

  <Tab title="Files" icon="folder">
    The **Files** tab stores documents and uploads related to the person.

    - Upload new files such as consent forms, medical letters, ID documents, or staff certifications.

    - Edit file details like titles or notes so other staff understand what each document is.

    - Remove files that are outdated or no longer needed, based on your camp’s retention policies.

    Files here stay tied to the profile, so staff can quickly pull up documents during check-in, staff onboarding, or emergencies.

    <Callout kind="tip" collapsed="false">
      Keep file names and notes specific, such as "Asthma action plan - 2025" or "Lifeguard certification - expires Aug 2026," so other staff can find what they need quickly.
    </Callout>
  </Tab>

  <Tab title="Medications" icon="shield">
    The **Medications** tab appears when your camp uses the medications feature.

    - Record active medications, dosages, timing, and any special instructions.

    - Note allergies, medical alerts, or emergency protocols that health staff need to see at a glance.

    - Update medications when families report changes before or during the season.

    Use this tab together with your camp’s health-center processes, and make sure only appropriate staff update these details.

    <Callout kind="alert" collapsed="false">
      Medication and allergy information is sensitive health data. Follow your camp’s privacy and medical-handling policies and limit access to staff who need this information to care for the person.
    </Callout>
  </Tab>

  <Tab title="Pickup" icon="settings">
    The **Pickup** tab shows who is allowed to pick up the camper and any related restrictions.

    - Review the list of approved adults and their contact details during check-in and checkout.

    - Add or remove pickup contacts when families update who is allowed to pick up the camper.

    - Record notes about custody considerations, photo ID checks, or specific pickup instructions.

    This tab helps front-desk and cabin staff verify that the person picking up a camper matches what the family has authorized.

    <Callout kind="info" collapsed="false">
      Pickup rules can be sensitive, especially in custody situations. Follow your camp’s legal and safety guidelines before changing who is allowed to pick up a camper.
    </Callout>
  </Tab>

  <Tab title="Signatures" icon="edit">
    The **Signatures** tab tracks signatures collected through forms and agreements.

    - See which forms have completed signatures and when they were signed.

    - Open signed items to confirm that the correct guardian or staff member signed.

    - Use signature timestamps to confirm when a family agreed to terms, waivers, or policies.

    This tab is useful when families or staff question whether they agreed to a policy or when you need to demonstrate consent for audits.

    <Callout kind="info" collapsed="false">
      Treat signature records as legal documents. Do not edit or share screenshots of this information outside your camp’s approved processes.
    </Callout>
  </Tab>

  <Tab title="Logins" icon="monitor">
    The **Logins** tab lists recent sign-ins tied to the family or person.

    - Review login timestamps and, when available, basic device or location details.

    - Confirm whether a parent or staff member actually logged in when they said they did.

    - Use the history to investigate access issues, such as many failed attempts or sign-ins from unexpected places.

    This tab helps support staff troubleshoot account access questions and verify how and when someone used the portal or app.
  </Tab>

  <Tab title="Audit" icon="history">
    The **Audit** tab shows a record of important changes made in the profile.

    - See who changed key information, such as contact details, balances, or medical notes, and when they made the change.

    - Review sequences of changes if a value looks wrong or you need to understand how a record evolved.

    - Use the history to answer questions about why a field has a certain value or to support internal reviews.

    <Callout kind="tip" collapsed="false">
      When you spot a confusing value on another tab, check the Audit tab to see when it changed and which staff member updated it before you reach out with questions.
    </Callout>
  </Tab>
</Tabs>

## Troubleshooting

Use these quick answers when something on a profile does not look right.

<ExpandableGroup>
  <Expandable title="I do not see the Sales, Forms, or Files tab" default-open="false">
    Some tabs are hidden based on your role or the features your camp uses.

    - If you still expect to see a tab, contact a system admin and ask them to review your permissions.
  </Expandable>

  <Expandable title="I do not see Pickup, Signatures, Logins, or Audit" default-open="false">
    - Look for a **More** menu or overflow area near the tab row; some layouts group less-used tabs there.

    - If you never see these tabs on any profile, your role may not include access or your camp may have turned off certain features.

    - If another staff member can see a tab that you cannot, ask a system admin to compare your permissions and update your access if appropriate.
  </Expandable>

  <Expandable title="A change did not save" default-open="false">
    Many fields on the Profiles screen save automatically when you move your cursor away or leave the field.

    - After updating a value, click or tap somewhere else on the page and watch for any small save indicator or confirmation.

    - If you close the browser tab immediately after typing, the change may not have time to save, especially on a slow connection.

    - If you still do not see the update, refresh the profile. If the old value returns, try the change again and confirm your internet connection.
  </Expandable>

  <Expandable title="Profile photo will not update" default-open="false">
    Profile photos come from the image you upload in the header area.

    - Make sure the file you upload follows your camp’s guidelines for size and type.

    - After uploading, use the crop and rotate tools, then confirm or save the new image when prompted.

    - If the old image still shows after a refresh, try clearing your browser cache or opening the profile in a different browser.

    - If no one can update photos, report the issue to your system admin so they can escalate the problem to your Campium contact.
  </Expandable>

  <Expandable title="What does Active Cart mean?" default-open="false">
    The **Active Cart** indicator means the family currently has items in their online cart that they have not checked out yet.

    - If a family says "I checked out but it still shows in the cart," first confirm whether you see a new sale and payment on the **Sales** tab.

    - If you do not see a matching sale, ask the family to confirm they reached a final confirmation screen and that they did not stop on a payment entry step.

    - You can suggest that they sign back in, open their cart, and either complete checkout or remove items they no longer want. If problems continue, capture details like time, browser, and device and share them with your system admin.
  </Expandable>
</ExpandableGroup>
