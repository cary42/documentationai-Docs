## What scheduling is for

Scheduling helps you decide who works which shifts and when. Use it to see coverage at a glance, avoid gaps, and make sure the right people are in the right place at the right time.

The scheduler is designed for office and admin staff who plan staff coverage. You use it to view the current schedule, add or adjust shifts, handle time off, and prevent staff from being double-booked.

## What you can do in the Staff Scheduler

Use the Staff Scheduler to manage day-to-day staffing without leaving the system:

- See who is working on any day, week, or month.
- Add, edit, move, copy, or cancel shifts.
- Set up repeating shifts, if available, to save time on common patterns.
- Record staff time off or unavailable time so you do not schedule over it.
- Publish schedule updates and choose who to notify.
- Review publish history to understand what staff have already seen.
- Spot and resolve conflicts so no one is double-booked.
- Print or share the schedule, if your screen offers these options.

## Understanding the scheduler screen

The main scheduling screen usually includes several areas that work together.

<Callout kind="info">
  Your screen may look slightly different depending on your setup. If you do not see one of these areas, it may not be available in your account.
</Callout>

### Date controls

Date controls let you move through the schedule.

- A **Today** button (if available) jumps back to the current day or week.
- Left and right arrows move backward or forward in time.
- A date picker opens a calendar so you can jump directly to a specific date.

When you change the date, the schedule grid updates to show that time frame.

### View options (Day, Week, Month)

View options change how much of the schedule you see at once.

- **Day view** focuses on one day, useful for detailed coverage and exact times.
- **Week view** shows the pattern across a full week, which helps spot gaps or overlaps.
- **Month view** (if available) shows a broad overview of shifts and time off.

Switch between views to match the type of planning you are doing.

### Filters (Role, Location, Staff)

Filters narrow the schedule to the people or areas you care about right now.

- **Role or Position** filters show only certain job types, such as instructors or front desk.
- **Location or Program** filters focus on one site or program at a time.
- **Staff** filters let you view a single person or a small group.

If you cannot find a shift, check whether a filter is hiding it.

### Schedule grid and shift blocks

The schedule grid shows time across the top or side, and staff, roles, or locations along the other side.

- Each **shift block** usually shows the staff name, start and end time, and sometimes role or location.
- Time off or unavailable time may appear in a different color or pattern so you can tell it apart from worked shifts.
- If your system supports it, conflict warnings may appear on or near shift blocks.

You use this grid to add new shifts, open existing ones, and see coverage.

### Toolbars and actions

The scheduling toolbar often includes common actions:

- Buttons for **Add shift**, **Publish**, **Print**, or **More actions**.
- Icons for **filters**, **view options**, or **publish history**.
- Menus that include options like **Copy schedule** or **Templates**, if available.

If you are not sure what a button does, hover over it to see a label, if your browser supports it.

## Viewing the schedule

Use the schedule view to see who is working on a given day, week, or time range.

<Callout kind="info">
  Your screen may show different views such as **Day**, **Week**, or **Month**, and may include filters like **Role**, **Location**, or **Program**.
</Callout>

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open the scheduling area" icon="monitor" titleType="p">
    Go to the staff scheduling area from your main navigation. Look for a label such as **Scheduling**, **Staff Schedule**, or **Shifts**.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Choose your view" icon="settings" titleType="p">
    Use the buttons or tabs at the top to switch between **Day**, **Week**, or **Month** views, if available. Pick the view that makes it easiest to see coverage.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Adjust the date range" icon="calendar" titleType="p">
    Use the arrows or date picker to move forward or backward in time. Confirm you are looking at the correct week or day before making changes.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Filter the schedule (optional)" icon="filter" titleType="p">
    If your screen supports it, use filters such as **Role**, **Location**, or **Staff member** to narrow the schedule to the group you care about right now.
  </Step>
</Steps>

When the schedule loads, you should see staff names alongside their shifts, with start and end times, and possibly the role or location for each shift.

## Adding a shift

Add a shift when you assign a staff member to work at a specific time.

<Callout kind="tip">
  Before you add a shift, decide who should work it, where they will work, and the start and end times. This reduces changes later.
</Callout>

<Steps>
  <Step title="Start a new shift" icon="plus" titleType="p">
    In the schedule view, select the day and time where you want to add a shift. You may click on an empty time slot or use an **Add shift** or **New shift** button.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Select the staff member" icon="users" titleType="p">
    Choose the staff member from the **Staff** or **Employee** dropdown. Confirm you have the correct person, especially if you have staff with similar names.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Set the shift time" icon="clock" titleType="p">
    Enter the **Start time** and **End time**. Some screens also allow you to set the **Date** or **Days** if the shift repeats. Make sure the times match what you agreed with the staff member.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Add details like role or location" icon="settings" titleType="p">
    You may see fields such as **Role**, **Position**, **Location**, or **Notes**. Fill in these fields to make it clear what the shift is for and where the staff member should be.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Save the shift" icon="check-circle" titleType="p">
    Select **Save**, **Create**, or a similar button. Confirm that the new shift now appears on the schedule in the correct place for that staff member.
  </Step>
</Steps>

If the shift does not appear as expected, refresh your view or check that your filters are not hiding it.

### Creating repeating shifts (if available)

If your scheduling screen offers repeating or recurring shifts, use them to set up regular patterns, such as a weekly class or standing front desk coverage.

<Callout kind="info">
  Look for options like **Repeat**, **Repeats weekly**, **Repeat on days**, or **Copy to future** when you create or edit a shift.
</Callout>

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open a shift for repeat setup" icon="edit" titleType="p">
    Add a new shift or open an existing shift that you want to repeat.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Turn on repeating options" icon="settings" titleType="p">
    If available, select a **Repeat** or **Recurring** option. Choose how often the shift repeats, such as every week, every other week, or on specific weekdays.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Set the start and end of the pattern" icon="calendar" titleType="p">
    Choose when the repeating pattern should begin and when it should stop. For example, you may repeat from the start of a season until a certain end date.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Review and save the repeating shifts" icon="check-circle" titleType="p">
    Before you save, check that the summary of the repeating pattern matches what you expect. After saving, scan the next few weeks to confirm the shifts appear correctly.
  </Step>
</Steps>

If you later need to change or stop a repeating shift, open one of the shifts and look for options to update **Only this shift** or **This and future shifts**, if offered.

## Editing a shift

Edit a shift whenever times, roles, or locations change.

<Callout kind="info">
  Some systems track changes to shifts. Make sure you only edit shifts you are allowed to change, especially close to the shift start time.
</Callout>

<Steps>
  <Step title="Find the shift you need to change" icon="search" titleType="p">
    In the schedule view, locate the staff member and the day with the shift you want to adjust.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Open the shift details" icon="edit" titleType="p">
    Select the shift. You may click on the shift block or use an **Edit** icon or menu option such as **Edit shift**.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Update the information" icon="settings" titleType="p">
    Adjust fields such as **Start time**, **End time**, **Role**, **Location**, or **Notes**. If the shift should move to a different staff member and your screen allows it, change the **Staff** field.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Handle repeating shifts carefully (if applicable)" icon="alert-triangle" titleType="p">
    If you are editing a shift that is part of a repeating pattern and you see options such as **Only this shift** or **All future shifts**, choose the option that matches your intent so you do not change past or unrelated dates by mistake.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Save your changes" icon="check-circle" titleType="p">
    Select **Save**, **Update**, or similar. Confirm that the schedule now shows the updated time, role, or staff member.
  </Step>
</Steps>

If your system supports drag-and-drop, you may move a shift directly on the calendar. After moving it, check that the times and any conflict warnings still look correct.

### Moving and resizing shifts (if available)

If your scheduler allows drag-and-drop, you can quickly adjust shifts without opening the full details.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Move a shift to a new time or staff member" icon="arrow-right" titleType="p">
    Click and hold the shift block, then drag it to a new time slot, day, or staff row, if the screen supports this. Release it where you want the shift to land.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Resize a shift to change its length" icon="edit" titleType="p">
    If available, drag the top or bottom edge of the shift block to shorten or extend the shift time. Watch the displayed start and end times as you drag.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Confirm the new details" icon="check-circle" titleType="p">
    After moving or resizing, check that the new time, date, and staff member (if changed) are correct, and that no unexpected conflict warnings appear.
  </Step>
</Steps>

If drag-and-drop does not work on your screen, use the standard edit steps instead.

## Removing or canceling a shift

Remove or cancel a shift when it should no longer be worked.

<Callout kind="alert">
  Removing a shift may affect payroll, staffing, and communication with staff. Confirm that you are canceling the correct shift before you continue.
</Callout>

<Steps>
  <Step title="Locate the shift to remove" icon="search" titleType="p">
    Use the schedule view to find the exact shift you want to remove, checking the staff name, date, and time.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Open the shift actions" icon="settings" titleType="p">
    Select the shift to open its details. Look for a **Delete**, **Remove**, or **Cancel shift** option, which may appear as a button or in a menu.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Confirm the removal" icon="trash" titleType="p">
    If a confirmation message appears, read it and then confirm you want to remove the shift. The shift should disappear from the schedule for that staff member.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Notify the staff member if needed" icon="info" titleType="p">
    If your system does not automatically notify staff about changes, contact the staff member to let them know the shift was canceled or reassigned.
  </Step>
</Steps>

If you remove a shift by accident, check whether your system allows you to undo the change or re-add the shift with the same details.

## Copying or reusing a schedule (if available)

If your scheduler includes copy or template tools, use them to save time when your schedule follows a regular pattern.

<Callout kind="tip">
  Copying a schedule works best when most shifts stay the same from week to week and only a few details change.
</Callout>

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open copy or template options" icon="settings" titleType="p">
    In the scheduling toolbar or menu, look for options such as **Copy day**, **Copy week**, **Duplicate shifts**, or **Templates**.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Choose what to copy" icon="calendar" titleType="p">
    If available, pick whether you want to copy a single day, a full week, or a saved template. Then select the date range you want to copy from and the one you want to copy to.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Review copied shifts" icon="monitor" titleType="p">
    After copying, scan the target days or weeks to confirm that shifts appear for the correct staff, roles, and locations. Adjust any exceptions, such as holidays or special events.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Save and plan to publish" icon="check-circle" titleType="p">
    Once you are satisfied with the copied schedule, make any last changes and plan when you will publish so staff see the new shifts.
  </Step>
</Steps>

If you find you are making the same changes every time you copy, consider creating a smaller template or pattern and applying it more selectively, if your system supports templates.

## Publishing the schedule

Publishing makes your latest changes visible to staff. Until you publish, staff cannot see new shifts or changes you have made.

<Callout kind="info">
  Think of publishing as sending out a new edition of the schedule. You can make many edits in the background, then publish once when you are ready to share them.
</Callout>

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open the publish option" icon="play-circle" titleType="p">
    In the scheduling toolbar, select **Publish**. The button usually shows how many unpublished shifts you have, such as **Publish (5)**.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Choose who to notify" icon="users" titleType="p">
    When the publish options appear, pick how you want to notify staff about this update.

    - **Only notify staff with new or changed shifts** — Sends an email only to people whose shifts were added or changed.
    - **Notify everyone with upcoming shifts** — Sends an email to everyone who has a shift in the schedule.
    - **Do not notify anyone** — Publishes the schedule without sending any emails.

  </Step>

  <Step title="Publish the schedule" icon="check-circle" titleType="p">
    Select **Publish** to confirm. Staff can now see the updated schedule, and any emails you chose to send will go out based on the option you selected.
  </Step>
</Steps>

If staff report that they cannot see a change you made, check whether you have published since making that change.

### Understanding unpublished vs published changes

Unpublished changes exist only in the scheduler for admins; published changes are what staff see.

- When you add or edit shifts, those updates may stay in an **unpublished** state until you publish.
- The number on the **Publish** button, if shown, helps you track how many changes are waiting.
- Publishing does not remove older schedule information; it layers your new edits onto what staff see next.

If you expect many last-minute edits, you may choose to publish more often but with more focused notifications.

## Viewing publish history

Use publish history to see who published the schedule, when they did it, and who was notified.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open publish history" icon="clock" titleType="p">
    In the scheduling toolbar, select the clock or history icon. This opens the list of past publishes.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Review past publishes" icon="info" titleType="p">
    Look through the list to see each publish, including the **Date**, who published it, how many shifts were included, and who was emailed.
  </Step>
</Steps>

If there is confusion about which version of the schedule staff received, use this list to confirm the most recent publish and who it reached.

## Handling time off or unavailable time (if available)

If your scheduling screen supports time off or unavailable time, use it to block times when staff cannot work.

<Callout kind="info">
  You may see options such as **Time off**, **Unavailable**, **PTO**, or **Leave**. These often work like shifts but show staff are not available.
</Callout>

<Steps>
  <Step title="Start a new time off entry" icon="plus" titleType="p">
    From the schedule or staff view, look for a button such as **Add time off**, **Mark unavailable**, or similar. You may also open a staff member and choose a **Time off** tab.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Choose the staff member and dates" icon="users" titleType="p">
    Select the staff member if not already selected. Enter the **Start date** and **End date**, or pick the specific day and times when they are unavailable.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Add details and reason" icon="info" titleType="p">
    You may see fields for **Reason** (for example, vacation or sick), **Location**, or **Notes**. Fill these in to help others understand why the staff member is not available.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Save the time off" icon="check-circle" titleType="p">
    Select **Save** or **Add**. Confirm that the schedule now shows the staff member as unavailable or blocked out for those times.
  </Step>
</Steps>

If you later approve or deny time off in another area, return to the schedule to confirm that shifts match the final decision and do not overlap with denied time off.

### Using time off to avoid scheduling mistakes

Time off entries are only useful if you rely on them while scheduling.

- Check the schedule for time off before assigning extra shifts to a staff member.
- When you see a conflict between a shift and time off, adjust the shift or reassign coverage.
- Use notes to record important details, such as whether time off is paid or unpaid, if that matters for your policies.

## Preventing conflicts and double-booking

Use the schedule and any built-in warnings to avoid overlapping shifts and double-booked staff.

<Callout kind="tip">
  Many systems show conflict warnings, such as colored borders, icons, or messages if a staff member is scheduled for two places at once.
</Callout>

To reduce conflicts:

- Check a staff member's full day or week view before adding a new shift.
- Watch for any warning icons or messages when you save a shift.
- Avoid giving one staff member overlapping **Start time** and **End time** ranges, even across different locations.
- Keep time off entries up to date so you do not schedule staff who are on leave.

If the system warns you about a conflict:

- Read the message to see which other shift is affected.
- Decide whether the conflict is accidental or allowed in special cases, such as on-call coverage.
- Open the new and existing shifts (or time off entries) to see the full picture before changing anything.

If the conflict is not allowed:

- Adjust the new shift times so they no longer overlap.
- Reassign one of the shifts to a different staff member.
- Remove a shift if coverage is not needed after all.

If the overlap is allowed in your policies, keep both shifts and add a clear note so others understand why the conflict is intentional.

After resolving a conflict, scan the schedule again to confirm the staff member is scheduled in only one active shift per time period, unless you intentionally allowed an overlap.

## Handling last-minute changes

Last-minute changes are common. Handle them in a way that keeps staff informed and the schedule accurate.

<Callout kind="alert">
  When you update the schedule close to a shift start time, staff might already be on their way. Make sure you clearly communicate urgent changes.
</Callout>

<Steps>
  <Step title="Adjust the shift or coverage" icon="edit" titleType="p">
    Edit, move, or cancel the affected shift as soon as you know about the change. If someone is calling out, reassign the shift to another staff member or shorten it to match partial coverage.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Publish if staff rely on the online schedule" icon="play-circle" titleType="p">
    If your staff check the online schedule, publish the change so they see it. Consider notifying **Only staff with new or changed shifts** so you do not flood others with messages.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Use additional communication if needed" icon="users" titleType="p">
    For urgent changes, use other tools such as **Mass Emails**, **SMS**, or **Push Notifications** to reach staff quickly, especially if they may not see a schedule update in time.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Add notes for context" icon="info" titleType="p">
    Add a short note on the shift explaining the reason for the change, such as a sick call, weather issue, or special event, so others understand why the schedule looks different.
  </Step>
</Steps>

After the hectic period passes, review the affected day or week to ensure the final schedule matches what actually happened.

## Troubleshooting common scheduling issues

<Callout kind="info">
  Use this section when something on the schedule does not look right or you cannot make the change you expect.
</Callout>

<ExpandableGroup>
  <Expandable title="I cannot see a shift I just added" default-open="true">

  - Check that you are on the correct **Date**, **Week**, or **Month**.
  - Clear or adjust filters such as **Role**, **Location**, or **Staff** that might be hiding the shift.
  - Refresh the schedule view and look again for the staff member and time.
  - Confirm that you saved the shift, and that it did not show an error or conflict message when you tried to save.

  </Expandable>

  <Expandable title="The system says there is a conflict or overlap">

  - Read the message to see which other shift or time off entry is affected.
  - Open the other shift or time off and adjust times so they no longer overlap.
  - If the overlap is allowed in your policies, confirm you want to keep both and note the reason in the **Notes** field.
  - If you believe the conflict warning is incorrect, double-check the staff member's full day or week to confirm how many shifts they have.

  </Expandable>

  <Expandable title="I cannot edit or delete a shift">

  - Make sure you have permission to change that staff member's schedule.
  - Check whether the shift is in the past or already approved for payroll; some systems lock these shifts.
  - Try editing a different shift to see if the issue is with your access or with that specific shift.
  - If needed, contact an admin or supervisor who has permission to adjust locked shifts.

  </Expandable>

  <Expandable title="Time off is not showing on the schedule">

  - Confirm that time off is turned on in your system, if that setting exists.
  - Check the staff member's time off dates and times for mistakes.
  - Refresh the schedule and ensure no filters are hiding time off entries.
  - Make sure you are looking at the same location or program as the time off entry, if those options are used in your setup.

  </Expandable>

  <Expandable title="Staff say their schedule looks different from mine">

  - Check the **Publish** button to see if there are unpublished changes that staff have not seen yet.
  - Review **Publish history** to confirm when the last publish occurred and who was notified.
  - Make sure staff are viewing the correct date range and that they do not have filters hiding some shifts.
  - If needed, publish again and notify staff with new or changed shifts so everyone has the same information.

  </Expandable>
</ExpandableGroup>

## Good habits for accurate schedules

Keep these habits in mind to maintain a clean, reliable staff schedule:

- Review the upcoming week at least once to confirm coverage and spot gaps or overlaps.
- Update shifts as soon as you know about changes, rather than waiting until the last minute.
- Record time off and unavailable time before you assign new shifts.
- Use notes on shifts for anything unusual, such as coverage swaps or special duties.
- Periodically check with staff to confirm the schedule matches their understanding.
- Plan your publish times so staff know when to expect new schedules, such as always publishing the next week by a certain day.
- Avoid making large changes without publishing; long batches of unpublished edits can confuse staff.
- After copying or repeating shifts, spot-check a few days at random to catch small mistakes.
- Use filters to review each location, role, or staff member in turn and confirm there are no double-bookings.
- Keep communication channels, like email addresses and phone numbers, up to date so staff can receive schedule notifications and urgent updates.
