## What the Attendance Kiosk does

The Attendance Kiosk helps staff quickly check campers in and out by scanning a QR code or entering a PIN. It is designed for fast line flow, clear visibility, and fewer attendance mistakes at busy drop-off and pick-up times.

The kiosk also supports optional pickup tracking, so you can record who picked up each camper when they check out.

## Before you start

Configure kiosk settings in the Admin site before staff use the Attendance Kiosk at the front desk.

<Steps>

<Step title="Turn on Kiosk Mode" icon="settings">

- In the Admin site, go to **Admin** > **Site Settings**.
- Find **Enable Kiosk Mode**.
- Turn **Enable Kiosk Mode** to **ON**.

</Step>

<Step title="Optional: Enable pickup tracking" icon="users">

- If you use authorized pickup tracking, make sure the **Pickup Module** is enabled.
- In **Site Settings**, turn on **Pickup Popup** to prompt staff to record who picked up the camper during check-out.

</Step>

</Steps>

<Callout kind="info">

When **Enable Kiosk Mode** is on, families see a **Show QR Code** option on the parent portal and can access their camper kiosk PIN.

</Callout>

## Where to find it

<Steps>

<Step title="Open the Attendance Kiosk" icon="folder">

- Go to **Reports** in your menu.
- Select the **System** reports group.
- Click **Attendance Kiosk**.

</Step>

</Steps>

## How staff use the kiosk

Staff use the Attendance Kiosk from the kiosk page to choose the right mode and check campers in and out.

<Steps>

<Step title="Choose mode and display options" icon="monitor">

- On the kiosk page, select **Check-in** or **Check-out** mode.
- Turn **Display Profile Info** on if you want to show a quick camper or family summary after each scan.

</Step>

<Step title="Scan QR code or enter PIN" icon="zap">

- Ask the family to show the camper QR code from the parent portal, then scan it with your device.
- If a QR code is not available, enter the camper's kiosk PIN instead.

</Step>

<Step title="Confirm the result" icon="check-circle">

- Review the result panel to confirm the correct camper and action.
- If something looks wrong, use the **Undo** button immediately.

</Step>

</Steps>

<Callout kind="tip">

Use QR codes as your primary method for speed and accuracy, and use PINs as a backup when a family does not have the QR code ready.

</Callout>

## Multiple sessions flow

When a camper is active in more than one session on the same day, the kiosk handles session selection after scanning.

After staff scan the QR code or enter the PIN, the kiosk shows the camper's eligible sessions as quick-select buttons. Staff tap the correct session, then tap **Check-in** or **Check-out** to record attendance.

<Callout kind="info">

You do not need to pre-select a session before scanning. The kiosk only asks for a session when the camper is active in more than one session that day.

</Callout>

## Check-out pickup selection

Pickup selection appears only when you use the pickup features and the kiosk is in Check-out mode.

When you are in **Check-out** mode and **Pickup Popup** is turned on, the kiosk shows the list of approved pickup people for that camper after you scan. Staff select the correct pickup person and then tap **Check-out** to record who picked up the camper.

<Callout kind="alert">

Train staff to always choose the correct pickup person before tapping **Check-out** so your pickup records stay accurate.

</Callout>

## Parent portal experience

Families access everything they need for the kiosk from the parent portal.

- Families can see each camper's **kiosk PIN**.
- Families can click **Show QR Code** to open the camper's unique QR code for quick scanning at drop-off and pick-up.

Encourage families to have the QR code ready on their phone when they arrive to keep lines moving.

## Undoing mistakes

Use the **Undo** button in the result panel to reverse the most recent kiosk action.

If staff record the wrong action or select the wrong camper or session, click **Undo** right away. This clears the last check-in or check-out so you can scan again with the correct information.

<Callout kind="tip">

Make **Undo** part of your front desk training so staff correct mistakes immediately, while the family is still at the desk.

</Callout>

## Best practices

Follow these practices to keep attendance accurate and lines moving quickly.

- **Confirm the mode before scanning.** Always check that the kiosk is in **Check-in** or **Check-out** as needed before scanning the next camper.
- **Keep Display Profile Info on during busy times.** The quick profile view helps staff confirm the right camper and avoid mix-ups when many families arrive at once.
- **Use QR as the default, PIN as backup.** QR codes are faster and reduce typing errors. Use PIN entry only when a QR code is not available.
- **Use Undo immediately if something looks off.** If staff notice the wrong camper, session, or pickup person, tap **Undo** and redo the scan while the family is still present.

## Common questions

<ExpandableGroup>

<Expandable title="Why am I asked to pick a session after scanning?">

When a camper is active in more than one session on the same day, the kiosk shows all eligible sessions as buttons after you scan. Staff must tap the correct session and then tap **Check-in** or **Check-out** to finish recording attendance.

</Expandable>

<Expandable title="Why am I asked to select a pickup person at check-out?">

In **Check-out** mode, if **Pickup Popup** is enabled and you use authorized pickup tracking, the kiosk shows the list of approved pickup people after you scan. Staff select who is picking up the camper so the system records the pickup person with the check-out.

</Expandable>

<Expandable title="Can we use this on a phone?">

Yes. You can use camera-based QR scanning from a phone-compatible browser.

</Expandable>

</ExpandableGroup>

## Device compatibility

The Attendance Kiosk runs in a browser, so you can use it on desktops, laptops, tablets, or phones.

If you use a phone, make sure the browser supports camera-based QR scanning so you can scan the camper QR code directly from the device.
